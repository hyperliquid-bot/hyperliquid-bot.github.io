Quick answer

A Hyperliquid grid bot automatically places staggered buy and sell orders across a price range, aiming to profit from oscillation in range-bound markets. It's a common third-party strategy on Hyperliquid, not a native product. Hyperliquid Bot can run a grid on the pairs and range you set, on a budget you control.

What is a grid bot?

A grid bot is an automated strategy that places laddered buy and sell orders across a defined price range.

Instead of trying to predict one perfect entry, the bot divides a range into levels. Near lower levels it can buy; near higher levels it can sell. If price keeps moving back and forth inside the range, the strategy attempts to turn that oscillation into repeated small fills. The core idea is simple, but execution discipline matters because every grid level, order size and stop condition changes the risk profile.

On Hyperliquid, a grid is best understood as third-party automation layered on top of the exchange. Hyperliquid Bot is independent and not affiliated with Hyperliquid; it runs the strategy you choose rather than creating a native exchange feature.

How a grid bot works on Hyperliquid

A Hyperliquid grid bot places orders across your selected range and manages them as price moves through the grid.

You choose the market, range, grid count and budget. The bot then places staggered orders according to that setup, replacing or managing orders as fills happen. Hyperliquid supports native exchange order behavior documented in Hyperliquid order types, while the grid logic itself sits in the bot. That distinction matters: the exchange executes orders, and the third-party tool decides when and where to place them.

Grid bots can run on Hyperliquid perps, but perps are leveraged instruments. Leverage can make a tight range more capital-efficient, but it also increases liquidation risk, and funding can affect open positions. A grid should therefore be sized as a risk-managed strategy, not as a set-and-forget profit machine.

When does a grid bot work best?

A grid bot works best when the market stays range-bound and keeps revisiting both sides of the grid.

Grid trading is a form of algorithmic trading: rules are set in advance, and software executes those rules without waiting for manual clicks. The favorable condition is a market that repeatedly rotates through the chosen band. In that environment, the bot may buy lower, sell higher, and continue working through the same price area.

The honest limitation is trend risk. If price breaks strongly upward, a conservative grid may sell too early and miss continuation. If price breaks strongly downward, the grid may keep accumulating exposure into weakness. That is why the range, budget, leverage and stop rules are not cosmetic settings. They are the strategy.

How to set up a grid on Hyperliquid

To set up a grid on Hyperliquid, choose the pair, define the range and grid count, set the budget, then enable the bot.

Grid setup checklist

  • Pick the Hyperliquid pair you want the grid to trade.
  • Set the lower and upper range, then choose the grid count.
  • Set the budget and position sizing limits you are comfortable risking.
  • Enable the strategy, monitor fills, and pause or adjust it when the market leaves the range.

The cleaner the setup, the easier it is to judge later. A grid should have a reason for its range, a known budget, and a clear condition for stopping. When you are ready, you can run a grid with Hyperliquid Bot from your own account.

Frequently asked questions

What is grid trading?

Grid trading is a strategy that places staggered buy and sell orders across a chosen price range, aiming to capture repeated movement inside that range.

Does a grid bot work on perps?

Yes. A grid bot can trade perpetual futures, but perps add leverage, funding, liquidation and market risk, so range and sizing matter.

Is a grid bot profitable?

No result is guaranteed. A grid bot depends on the range holding long enough for oscillation to offset costs and risk.

Is grid a native Hyperliquid feature?

No. Grid trading is a third-party strategy run on top of Hyperliquid, not a native Hyperliquid product. Hyperliquid Bot is independent and not affiliated with Hyperliquid.
