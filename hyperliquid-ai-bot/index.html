How is an AI bot different from a basic bot?

An AI bot uses adaptive or signal-assisted logic, while a basic bot usually follows fixed rules.

A basic bot might trade only when a simple condition is true: price crosses a level, funding reaches a threshold, or a grid interval fills. That can be useful because fixed rules are predictable and easy to audit. The tradeoff is that fixed rules can keep behaving the same way even when the market regime changes.

An AI-assisted bot adds interpretation around signals such as trend strength, volatility, funding conditions, or copy-trading inputs. That does not make it magic. It still follows a strategy, uses the data it can see, and can be wrong. The practical difference is that it can adjust sizing, entry timing, or trade selection more dynamically than a rigid rule set, while you still define the budget and stop conditions.

What are the limits and risks?

A Hyperliquid AI bot can automate execution, but it cannot remove leverage, liquidation, funding or market risk.

Hyperliquid perps are leveraged instruments. If a position moves against you, liquidation can occur; if funding changes, holding a position can become more expensive; if liquidity or volatility shifts, entries and exits can perform worse than expected. Hyperliquid's own Hyperliquid's risk overview is the right authority for the core exchange risks.

The honest role of an AI bot is discipline and speed, not certainty. It can help apply a strategy consistently, reduce manual reaction time, and avoid missed execution, but it cannot know the future or guarantee profit. Any product promising risk-free or guaranteed returns should be treated as a warning sign. If you want to use automation after understanding those limits, you can automate it with Hyperliquid Bot.
